## Theoretical Frameworks (COMPS)

| Framework | Role in Your Work |
|-----------|------------------|
| **EVT** (Eccles et al.) | Organizes MR strategies by target mechanism: expectancy / value / cost |
| **Metamotivation** (Miele, Scholer, Fujita) | Explains *why* MR effects are heterogeneous — strategy fit depends on metamotivational knowledge |
| **SRL** (Pintrich, Wolters) | Historical foundation; MR as a dimension of SRL |
| **SDT** (Deci & Ryan) | Autonomous vs. controlled regulation; one of 4 theoretical frameworks in RQ1b |
| **Volitional Control** (Corno) | Competing framework for MR boundary |

**Integrative framework:**
```
Metamotivation (upstream: monitoring + knowledge)
    ↓ guides strategy selection (situation-specific fit)
Motivational Regulation (strategies — grounded in SRL/SDT)
    ↓ acts on
EVT constructs (expectancy / value / cost)  ← EVT as mechanism framework,
    ↓ predicts                                 NOT as strategy taxonomy
STEM Outcomes (motivation / achievement / persistence / choices)
```

**EVT positioning (Feb 23 corrected):** EVT is NOT the organizing framework for MR
strategies (MR is grounded in SRL/SDT). EVT explains *what motivational states in STEM
need regulating* — the mechanism layer between strategies and outcomes.

## Key Constructs

| Construct | Definition | Key Source |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Motivational Regulation (MR) | Strategies to initiate/maintain motivation toward goals | Wolters (2003) |
| Metamotivational Monitoring | Detecting gaps between current motivation state and task demands | Miele & Scholer (2018) |
| Metamotivational Knowledge | Stored knowledge guiding strategy choice — 3 types: task / strategy / self | Miele et al. (2024) |
| Metamotivational Beliefs | Beliefs about task-motivation fit; accuracy measurable | Scholer et al. (2018) |
| Situation-specific fit | Whether selected MR strategy matches motivational demands | von der Mülbe et al. (2026) |
| Cost regulation | MR strategies targeting perceived cost — **no formal strategy named in literature** | EVT-derived gap |

**Conceptual hierarchy:**
- Metamotivational Beliefs (broad) ⊃ Metamotivational Knowledge (accurate subset)
- Metamotivation ≠ peer of MR; it is the *metacognitive prerequisite* for adaptive MR

### Key Gaps (6 identified)
1. **Domain specificity** — MR frameworks are domain-general; STEM cost structure unintegrated
2. **Metamotivational knowledge in STEM** — never tested in EVT-domain or STEM context (Teo 2025 = first real-world study, construal level only)
3. **Cost regulation** — 18 strategies in Villar (2024) all increase motivation; none target cost reduction
4. **Classroom microclimate** — Robinson's microclimate research never crossed with MR
5. **Developmental trajectory** — when/how does metamotivational capacity develop in STEM?
6. **STEM intervention** — no RCT testing MR training in STEM

### Key Literature (26 papers across 5 themes)
**Must read first (6 core):**
1. Wolters (2003) — MR founding definition
2. Miele, Fujita et al. (2024) — Metamotivation 3-type knowledge framework *(Motivation Science)*

### Metamotivation's Role: Explanation of MR Heterogeneity (Role B)
> "Metamotivation explains not whether students regulate their motivation, but whether they regulate it *adaptively*—providing a theoretical account for the heterogeneity in MR effectiveness documented in existing meta-analyses."

Empirical support: Teo et al. (2025) showed strategy discernment (= metamotivational knowledge) predicts grades **independently of strategy repertoire** — having strategies ≠ knowing when to use them.

### Future Empirical Study Direction
> *"Do college students in introductory STEM courses hold accurate metamotivational knowledge about EVT-relevant regulatory strategies—specifically, do they know when reducing perceived costs vs. enhancing value vs. bolstering expectancy is most adaptive—and does this knowledge predict their STEM persistence and motivation trajectories?"*
- Design: vignette paradigm (adapt Teo 2025) + LPA (Robinson method)
- Context: STEM gateway courses
- This is the dissertation study, not the COMPS
